Dark Work Factories Are Meth
============================
SUBTITLE TEXT

![](./images/image.png)

I once took a pill at a Carnival beach club. It's called _bala_ in Portuguese: bullet.

Supposedly it's MDMA.

I've taken MDMA before, but this time was different, like getting shot out of a fucking gun. I just **had** to dance, had to move, had to talk with people. Not wanted to - **HAD TO**.

I decided that the best thing to do would be approach random groups of strangers and ask them in broken Portuguese, "I'm the Saint of the Dance. Would you like a blessing?"

Nearly nobody said yes to the wild-eyed, drenched-in-sweat _louco_, but for the few who did I'd flail about in random dance movements for a second and then run off to the next group. Rejection didn't concern me whatsoever - they didn't want the blessing and that was fine. I had infinite power.

Then, as my brain desperately tried to grab homeostasis back from the insane chemicals in my veins, came the crash.

Paranoia. Anxiety. Total inability to sleep until it ambushed me for a few hours, then I'd wake up wired to pee a lake and repeat the cycle.

My guess now is that the pill was part meth.

Dark Work Factories
-------------------
Last year I read the Almanack of Naval Ravikant.

There's infinite wisdom there and you must read it, but what stayed with me is how essential leverage is.

I wrote [a bunch of posts as a result](leverage series), eventually realizing that [the goal in life is unique work](goal is unique work): anything that can't be uniquely done by you, you should [delegate or automate](be rembrandt).

Frustrated with how much manual labor I had to do to get a post published and inspired by [another crucial post](inputs not outpus), I started thinking about a "AI work factories".

You could, I supposed, have a work-tracking system (I use Todoist) and just shovel tasks at the AI. It'd figure out everything: coding, post-publishing, calendar management, social feed monitoring - whatever you need it to be.

I [started trying to build it with n8n](building the factory) with n8n, but that was way too tedious.

I tried using the MCP integrations of ChatGPT, but they sucked (couldn't even edit calendar events, wtf!).

Then I switched to building out Claude skills to generate Instagram video scripts, 

Then, on New Year's Day, Steve Yegge dropped [Gastown](gastown). "There it is!" I thought, "That's the factory!"

Only, Gastown is really a coding factory, and I'm not sold on all its complexity being necessary.

I wanted an AI _work_ factory, that could handle all work - be it coding or calendar management or workout planning.

So I started building what turned into [my AI work factory, AgenC](agenc).

I've since come to call this thing a [dark factory](dark factories): partly because there are no humans and partly because it sounds fucking cool.

Brain Drain
-----------
All the parts of building that I'm _so_ done with after coding for 20 years - the symbol-pushing, Googling syntax, debugging - is handled by the AI.

And then I started trying to drive 3, 4, 5 Claudes at once.

It's whack-a-mole, constant context-switching to keep the robots working.

It's Minecraft, where each improvement makes the system better to produce the next one.

It's Starcraft, juggling unit teams to accomplish missions.

It's all on my laptop, producing for me.

And it's doing fucking weird things to my brain.

I'd catch myself aimlessly walking around the apartment, infinitely restless, randomly talking off my shirt or touching things or flopping face-first down onto the bed only to immediately get up again.

I couldn't sleep because I had so many ideas, and when I did sleep I'd wake up in the middle of the night with a brain raging with ideas.

And now any idea that pops into my head must be captured in case an agent thrown at it yields fruit, which means I'm very easily distracted.

In short, it is just like that Carnival pill. I think it's making me ADHD.

I think I get meth-heads now. Your baseline dopamine is jacked so high that everything feels important and worth paying attention to. For a druggie, it's license plates revealing the secret to how the government is trying to control us. For me, it's ensuring I capture this idea _right now_ because it's all going to fit together in the grand plan of my AI dark factory.

It's not LLM psychosis. It's LLM mania.

Yegge called it [the AI vampire](ai vampire).

But it's not unexpected.

Fighting Back
-------------
Nicholas Carr's [The Shallows](the shallows) talks about how technology - even simple tech like books and clocks - reshapes our brains.

Carr focuses on the then-new internet - its links and summaries and distracted nonlinear browsing, and how our brains rewire to that pattern.

But the internet is small potatoes next to the always-on infinitely-replicable alien intelligences we have now.

If the internet connects, AI amplifies.

Suddenly it's foolish of me to brainstorm alone, because I could get 20 perspectives I didn't think of with AI.

And why settle for a human therapist who charges by the hour when I can have a conversation for as long as I need with Claude?

Suddenly if you're not thinking with AI, you're falling behind.

And I feel it.
