Building An AI Work Factory
===============
SUBTITLE TEXT

![](./images/image.png)

Hook:
- The future is you as the CEO of an organization of agents
- You can scale this org up and down infinitely
- The main work becomes organization-building:
    - ensuring that your organization has the necessary components and structure to get done the work you want to get done
    - getting problems that nobody else can deal with surfaced to you for you to fix

Background:
- Link to my previous posts talking about this
- Maybeee talk about my previous problems, where this came from?
    1. Publishing lots of Substack posts and thinking "man there HAS to be a better way"
    2. Cleaning through my Todoist inbox each day and thinking "I really wish I could just delegate work to an AI and have it get done"
- Reading the Inputs, Not Outputs post
- Realizing early this year that we don't have problems with DOING anymore.. AI is the infinite doer. What we want now are mind-readers.
    - When AI messes up, it's not because AI _can't_ do the right thing. It's because we underestimate how much context and how many assumptions we have going on.
    - It's an **alignment problem**
    - Therefore, the bulk of our work is going to be the slow, grindy work of encoding our assumptions and preferences into something AI-readable

How this started:
- I started trying to practice Inputs, Not Outputs by encoding all my preferences and such into Claude config (CLAUDE.md, settings.json, skills, etc.)
- But I started to spend tons of time cd'ing around getting in and out of the Claude sessions, doing the Git commits, approving tool requests, restarting Claudes with latest config, and juggling which window was doing what work
- First I started building aliases that helped in jumping around windows
- Then I discovered tmux, and how I could program new windows so I built a command palette
- And then it expanded and expanded

Now:
- Link to AgenC
- I spend my time firing off work, analyzing plans and results, and surfacing lessons that need to be further rolled back into the factory
- things that are handled for:
    - isolated workspaces + workspace cleanup
    - MCP server secrets injected from 1Password
    - Popping around sessions
- It's still not perfect:
    - I want better visibility into what work's been fired off
    - I want to have a task manager

What I've learned:
- We are headed to organizations of agents
